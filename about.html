About Me

My name is Caleb Kinjerski, and I am a Wisconsin native who has always enjoyed creating things. Growing up, I spent a lot of time outdoors, but I also developed a strong interest in technology, art, and problem-solving. Looking back, that combination of creativity and technical curiosity is what ultimately led me to pursue a degree in Computer Science.

Finding My Path

I grew up in a small blue-collar town where farming, football, and hard work were a big part of everyday life. While there is nothing wrong with that lifestyle, I always felt a desire to explore something different. I knew I wanted a career that allowed me to build, create, and continuously learn, but it took me a long time to figure out what that actually looked like.

During high school, I spent a lot of time in woodworking and metalworking classes because they gave me an outlet to create things with my own hands. I enjoyed the process of taking an idea and turning it into something tangible. At the time, I thought manufacturing and machining would be the natural next step, so after graduation I enrolled in a local technical college to pursue a degree in CNC machining and metalworking.

It did not take long for me to realize that path was not the right fit. While I enjoyed building things, I found myself wanting more creativity and problem-solving than the work typically allowed. Much of the focus was on producing the same parts repeatedly rather than creating something new. That experience forced me to take a step back and ask myself what I truly wanted to do instead of simply choosing what seemed practical at the time.

Discovering Computer Science

After reflection, I realized that Computer Science combined everything that interested me. It offered opportunities to create, solve problems, work with technology, and continuously improve my skills. Once I understood that, I returned to school to complete my general education courses before transferring to the University of Wisconsin–Oshkosh to begin pursuing a Computer Science degree.

Moving to Oshkosh was one of the biggest challenges I had faced at that point in my life. I was living on my own for the first time, working to support myself, and trying to balance school with the realities of adulthood. Many semesters required me to work significant hours while taking classes, which often meant juggling assignments, studying, and work schedules at the same time.

Like many people, my plans changed dramatically when COVID-19 arrived. With so much uncertainty surrounding work, housing, and education, I made the difficult decision to pause my college journey and move back home. At the time, it felt like a major setback, but it ultimately became a necessary step that allowed me to regain stability during an unpredictable period.

Building Experience

After the pandemic began to ease, I entered the workforce and worked in security and fire alarm installation. That role gave me valuable experience solving problems, working with technology, and thinking creatively in real-world situations.

Not long after, another opportunity presented itself. A friend offered me a position in industrial sales, which led me to move to Madison and begin a new chapter in my career. Over the last several years, I have grown professionally, taken on additional responsibilities, and developed strong communication, customer service, and problem-solving skills.

Even with that success, I never lost sight of my goal of completing my degree. I knew that Computer Science was still the path I wanted to pursue, and once I reached a point where I had the financial stability and work-life balance to make it happen, I decided it was time to finish what I started.

Completing the Journey

That decision eventually led me to Southern New Hampshire University. The flexibility of the program allowed me to continue working full time while completing my degree, something that would not have been possible earlier in my life. Over the last several years, I have worked steadily toward graduation while balancing my professional responsibilities and personal goals.

Today, I find myself approaching the finish line through the CS499 Capstone project. Looking back, the path to earning my degree was far from straightforward. There were career changes, financial challenges, unexpected setbacks, and moments where continuing forward would have been easy to walk away from. Instead, each challenge reinforced how important this goal was to me.

As I prepare to graduate, I am proud of how far I have come and excited for what comes next. While earning my degree marks the completion of a major chapter in my life, I view it as the beginning of a new one. Technology is constantly evolving, and I believe there will always be new skills to learn, new challenges to solve, and new opportunities to create something meaningful. I look forward to continuing that journey and applying everything I have learned to a career in software development.
